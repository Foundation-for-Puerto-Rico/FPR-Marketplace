## 1. General Procurement Standards (§200.318)

FPR must use its own documented procurement procedures that reflect applicable Federal, state, and local laws, provided they conform to 2 CFR 200.

Key requirements:
- Maintain written standards of conduct covering conflicts of interest and governing employee actions in the selection, award, and administration of contracts
- Ensure all procurement transactions are conducted in a manner providing full and open competition
- Maintain oversight of contractors to ensure performance
- Maintain records sufficient to detail the history of procurement (rationale for method, selection of contract type, contractor selection or rejection, basis for contract price)
- Use time and materials (T&M) contracts only after determining no other contract type is suitable AND with a ceiling price

### Written Procurement Procedures
FPR must maintain written procurement procedures that address:
- Method selection criteria based on dollar thresholds
- Competition requirements
- Conflict-of-interest policies
- Documentation requirements
- Review and approval authorities
- Contract administration and closeout

## 2. Competition Requirements (§200.319)

All procurement must be conducted providing full and open competition. Actions that restrict competition are prohibited:
- Placing unreasonable requirements on firms to qualify
- Requiring unnecessary experience or excessive bonding
- Noncompetitive pricing practices between firms or affiliates
- Organizational conflicts of interest
- Specifying a brand name instead of describing performance requirements
- Any arbitrary action in the procurement process

Geographic preferences are prohibited except where mandated by federal statute (e.g., Build America, Buy America for certain programs).

FPR must conduct appropriate analysis of lease versus purchase alternatives for property or equipment when feasible.

Solicitations must:
- Include a clear and accurate description of technical requirements
- Identify all requirements bidders must fulfill
- Identify all evaluation factors and their relative importance
- Not contain features that unduly restrict competition

## 3. Methods of Procurement (§200.320)

### Micro-Purchase (§200.320(a)(1))
- **Threshold:** ≤$10,000 (or lower if FPR's policy sets a lower limit)
- **Process:** No competitive quotations required
- **Requirements:** Purchases distributed equitably among qualified suppliers; price must be reasonable (compare to previous purchases, catalog prices, or market data)
- **Documentation:** Purchase record showing what, when, from whom, and price reasonableness

### Simplified Acquisition (§200.320(a)(2))
- **Threshold:** $10,001–$250,000
- **Process:** Obtain price or rate quotations from an adequate number of qualified sources (FPR may exercise judgment on what number is adequate — the 2024 revision removed the rigid "two or more" requirement)
- **Requirements:** Price/rate comparisons. Select the one most advantageous to the program considering price and other factors.
- **Documentation:** Quotations received, evaluation, selection rationale

### Sealed Bids / Formal Advertising (§200.320(b)(1))
- **Threshold:** >$250,000 (preferred for construction)
- **Conditions:** Complete specifications available, two or more responsible bidders expected, selection based principally on price
- **Process:** Publicly advertise, open bids publicly, firm fixed-price contract to lowest responsive and responsible bidder
- **Documentation:** Public notice, bid tabulation, award determination

### Competitive Proposals (§200.320(b)(2))
- **Threshold:** >$250,000 (when sealed bids not appropriate)
- **Process:** Publicize RFP, evaluate proposals against stated criteria, negotiate with offerors in the competitive range
- **Requirements:** Written evaluation criteria, independent cost estimate, cost or price analysis for every procurement above simplified threshold
- **Documentation:** RFP, proposals received, evaluation matrix, negotiation record, selection memo

### Noncompetitive Proposals / Sole Source (§200.320(c))
- **When permitted:**
  1. Item is available from only one source
  2. Public emergency (urgency won't allow competitive process)
  3. Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity expressly authorizes noncompetitive
  4. After solicitation from a number of sources, competition is determined inadequate
- **Documentation:** Must prepare and maintain a written sole source justification (see Section 13 below)

## 4. Contracting with Small and Minority Businesses (§200.321)

FPR must take affirmative steps to ensure that small businesses, minority-owned businesses, women-owned businesses, and (since 2024) veteran-owned businesses are used when possible. Steps include:
- Include qualified firms on solicitation lists
- Ensure solicitation allows them to participate
- Divide total requirements into smaller tasks when feasible
- Use delivery schedules that encourage participation
- Use services of SBA, Minority Business Development Agency, and state/local offices
- Require prime contractors to take similar steps for subcontracting

## 5. Domestic Preferences (§200.322)

FPR should, to the greatest extent practicable, provide a preference for purchasing goods, products, and materials produced in the United States. This includes manufactured products with substantial transformation in the US.

Note: Build America, Buy America Act (BABAA) requirements may apply to specific awards (particularly infrastructure-related programs like EDA and some CDBG-DR projects) with more stringent domestic content requirements.

## 6. Contract Cost and Price (§200.324)

FPR must perform:
- **Cost or price analysis** for every procurement over the simplified acquisition threshold ($250,000)
- **Independent cost estimate** before receiving bids or proposals for major procurements
- **Profit negotiation** as a separate element for each contract where there is no price competition and cost analysis is performed

FPR must not use cost-plus-a-percentage-of-cost or percentage-of-construction-cost methods of contracting.

## 7. Bonding Requirements (§200.326)

For construction or facility improvement contracts exceeding the simplified acquisition threshold ($250,000):
- **Bid guarantee** — 5% of bid price from each bidder
- **Performance bond** — 100% of contract price
- **Payment bond** — 100% of contract price

The awarding agency may accept lesser amounts at its discretion.

## 8. Contract Provisions (Appendix II)

All contracts made by FPR under federal awards must contain applicable provisions including:
- Remedies for breach
- Termination for cause and convenience
- Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)
- Davis-Bacon Act (for construction >$2,000 under certain programs)
- Copeland Anti-Kickback Act
- Contract Work Hours and Safety Standards Act
- Rights to inventions (if applicable)
- Clean Air Act and Federal Water Pollution Control Act (for contracts >$150,000)
- Debarment and suspension clause
- Byrd Anti-Lobbying Amendment (for contracts >$100,000)
- Record access for oversight agencies

## 9. Suspension and Debarment (§200.214)

Before every procurement action and subaward, FPR must verify that the entity is not excluded or disqualified by checking:
- **SAM.gov** (System for Award Management) — primary source
- Check at time of award and periodically during performance
- Include a certification clause in contracts and subawards

If an entity is listed as excluded on SAM.gov, FPR cannot award a contract or subaward to that entity.

## 10. Documentation Checklist

For every procurement, maintain a file containing:

**All Procurements:**
- [ ] Description of goods/services procured
- [ ] Procurement method used and justification for that method
- [ ] Contract type (firm fixed-price, cost-reimbursement, T&M)
- [ ] Basis for contractor selection
- [ ] Basis for cost/price (how reasonableness was determined)
- [ ] SAM.gov verification (screenshot or printout)
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest disclosure

**Simplified Acquisitions ($10,001–$250,000):**
- [ ] All of the above, plus:
- [ ] Quotations received from qualified sources
- [ ] Comparison/evaluation of quotes
- [ ] Selection rationale

**Formal Procurements (>$250,000):**
- [ ] All of the above, plus:
- [ ] Public advertisement/solicitation document (IFB or RFP)
- [ ] Independent cost estimate (prepared before receiving bids)
- [ ] Bid/proposal tabulation or evaluation matrix
- [ ] Cost or price analysis
- [ ] Negotiation memorandum (if applicable)
- [ ] Written determination for contract type selection
- [ ] Required contract provisions (Appendix II)
- [ ] Bonding documentation (if construction)

## 11. Sole Source Justification Guide

When noncompetitive procurement is necessary, prepare a written justification that includes:

1. **Description** — What is being procured and estimated cost
2. **Basis for sole source** — Which of the four permissible conditions applies:
   - Only one source (explain why no alternatives exist)
   - Public emergency (explain urgency and why competitive process is infeasible)
   - Federal agency authorization (attach written authorization)
   - Inadequate competition (document the failed solicitation effort)
3. **Market research** — What steps were taken to identify alternatives
4. **Price reasonableness** — How was price determined to be fair (prior purchases, published price lists, independent estimate)
5. **Unique qualifications** — Why this specific vendor (proprietary technology, expertise, continuity of service)
6. **Duration** — How long the sole source arrangement will last
7. **Approval** — Signature of authorized official

## 12. Conflict of Interest Requirements (§200.318(c))

FPR must maintain written standards of conduct that:

- Prohibit employees engaged in procurement from having real or apparent conflicts of interest
- Cover organizational conflicts of interest (e.g., FPR cannot contract with an entity in which an FPR officer, employee, or board member has a financial interest)
- Require disclosure of potential conflicts
- Include disciplinary actions for violations

**No FPR employee, officer, or agent may:**
- Participate in the selection, award, or administration of a contract if a real or apparent conflict of interest exists
- This includes situations where the employee, their family, partner, or organization has a financial or other interest in the contractor

**Gifts:** FPR employees, officers, and agents must not solicit or accept gratuities, favors, or anything of monetary value from contractors or potential contractors. FPR may set minimum thresholds where the financial interest is not substantial or the gift is unsolicited and of nominal value.
