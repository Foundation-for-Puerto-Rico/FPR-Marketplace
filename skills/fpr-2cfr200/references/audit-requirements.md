## 1. Single Audit Threshold (§200.501)

**Threshold (2024 revision):** Non-federal entities that expend **$1,000,000 or more** in federal awards during their fiscal year must have a Single Audit or program-specific audit.

Previous threshold (pre-October 1, 2024 for audit purposes): $750,000.

**If FPR expends less than $1,000,000** in federal awards: No Single Audit required, but records must be available for review and FPR is not exempt from other 2 CFR 200 requirements.

**If FPR expends $1,000,000 or more:** Single Audit required, conducted by an independent auditor in accordance with Government Auditing Standards (Yellow Book) and the OMB Compliance Supplement.

## 2. Basis for Determining Federal Awards Expended (§200.502)

Federal awards expended includes:
- All federal awards, whether received directly or as a subrecipient
- The value of federal awards provided to subrecipients
- Loan or loan guarantee amounts for new loans during the fiscal year
- Interest subsidies, insurance, and other non-cash programs

For FPR: Include expenditures from WCRP (CDBG-DR), Bottom Up DRI (EDA), PULSO (SBA), Community Innovation Hub (USDA), and any other federal awards or pass-through funds.

## 3. Frequency of Audits (§200.504)

Audits must be performed annually, covering the auditee's fiscal year. The first year of operations may cover a shorter period.

The audit must be completed and submitted to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC) within **nine months** after the end of the fiscal year (or the audit period). Extensions are possible with cognizant agency approval.

## 4. Auditee Responsibilities (§200.508)

FPR must:
- Identify all federal awards received and expended, including the CFDA/ALN title and number, federal award identification number, fiscal year, name of federal agency, and name of pass-through entity
- Maintain internal controls over federal programs that provide reasonable assurance of compliance
- Comply with federal statutes, regulations, and award terms
- Evaluate and monitor compliance
- Take prompt corrective action on audit findings
- Prepare the SEFA (Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards)

## 5. Auditor Selection (§200.509)

FPR must procure its audit services following the procurement standards of §§200.317–200.327. The auditor must:
- Be a licensed CPA or an audit organization meeting applicable standards
- Meet the independence standards of Government Auditing Standards (Yellow Book)
- Not be the same firm providing non-audit services that would impair independence

The audit engagement must comply with Government Auditing Standards (GAGAS / Yellow Book), issued by the Comptroller General.

## 6. Audit Scope and Reporting (§§200.514–200.516)

### Financial Statements
The auditor must determine whether the financial statements are presented fairly in all material respects in accordance with GAAP (or OCBOA if applicable).

### Internal Controls
The auditor must obtain an understanding of internal controls pertinent to the financial statements and to each major program, and must test internal controls.

### Compliance
For each major program, the auditor must:
- Understand the compliance requirements in the OMB Compliance Supplement
- Plan and perform audit procedures to obtain reasonable assurance about compliance
- Issue an opinion on whether FPR complied with the types of compliance requirements that could have a direct and material effect on each major program

### Major Program Determination
The auditor identifies "major programs" based on a risk-based approach considering:
- Dollar threshold (Type A programs: expenditures ≥ the larger of $750,000 or 3% of total federal awards)
- Risk assessment (current and prior audit results, oversight by agencies, program complexity)

## 7. Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA)

FPR must prepare the SEFA as a supplementary schedule to the financial statements. The SEFA must include:

| Required Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Federal grantor agency | Name of the federal agency (HUD, EDA, SBA, USDA) |
| Pass-through entity | If subaward — name of pass-through (e.g., PRDOH for WCRP) |
| CFDA/ALN number | Assistance Listing Number for each program |
| Federal award identification | Award or grant number |
| Federal expenditures | Amount expended during the fiscal year |
| Total federal awards expended | Sum of all federal expenditures |

Additional required disclosures:
- Accounting policies used in preparing the SEFA
- Whether the de minimis indirect cost rate was used
- Loans or loan guarantees outstanding at year-end

The SEFA may be prepared on the basis of GAAP or OCBOA (per 2024 revision).

## 8. Types of Audit Findings

Audit findings are categorized by severity:

### Material Weakness
A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in internal control such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material noncompliance will not be prevented or detected on a timely basis.

### Significant Deficiency
A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in internal control that is less severe than a material weakness but important enough to merit the attention of those charged with governance.

### Material Noncompliance
Noncompliance with a compliance requirement that could have a direct and material effect on a major program. Results in a modified (qualified, adverse, or disclaimer) opinion on compliance.

### Known Questioned Costs
Costs the auditor has identified as appearing to violate a provision of a law, regulation, or award term. The auditor must report known questioned costs greater than $25,000.

### Common FPR Risk Areas for Findings
- Inadequate time-and-effort documentation for split-funded employees
- Missing procurement documentation (especially sole source justification)
- Late or incomplete financial/performance reporting
- Insufficient subrecipient monitoring documentation
- Commingling of federal and non-federal funds
- Costs charged outside the period of performance
- Missing SAM.gov verification for contractors/subrecipients
- Inadequate segregation of duties

## 9. Corrective Action Plans

When audit findings are reported, FPR must prepare a corrective action plan that:
- Addresses each finding
- Identifies the responsible official
- Describes planned corrective actions
- Provides a timeline for implementation
- If FPR disagrees with a finding, explains the reasons

The corrective action plan is submitted as part of the reporting package to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse.

## 10. Management Decisions (§200.521)

The federal awarding agency or pass-through entity must issue a management decision on audit findings within **six months** of acceptance of the audit report.

The management decision must:
- Clearly state whether the finding is sustained, not sustained, or additional information is needed
- Describe the reasons for the decision
- Describe expected corrective actions and a timetable
- For sustained findings involving questioned costs, state whether costs are allowed or disallowed

FPR (as a pass-through for WCRP subrecipients) has the same obligation to issue management decisions on findings related to its subawards.

## 11. Audit Resolution Timeline

| Milestone | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Fiscal year end | Day 0 |
| Audit completed and submitted to FAC | Within 9 months |
| Corrective action plan prepared | At time of audit report |
| Management decision issued | Within 6 months of audit acceptance |
| Corrective actions implemented | Per timeline in corrective action plan |
| Follow-up audit verification | Next annual audit cycle |

## 12. FPR Audit Preparation Checklist

**Financial Management:**
- [ ] General ledger reconciled and up to date
- [ ] Federal awards tracked separately by program/CFDA number
- [ ] Cash receipts and disbursements properly documented
- [ ] Bank reconciliations current
- [ ] Journal entries properly documented and approved

**SEFA Preparation:**
- [ ] All federal awards identified (direct and pass-through)
- [ ] CFDA/ALN numbers verified for each program
- [ ] Award numbers and award dates documented
- [ ] Expenditures reconciled to general ledger
- [ ] Pass-through entity information included for subawards

**Personnel and Effort:**
- [ ] Time-and-effort records for all federally-funded employees
- [ ] Split-funded employees have documented allocation methodology
- [ ] Payroll records reconciled to time documentation
- [ ] Fringe benefit rates calculated and documented

**Procurement:**
- [ ] Procurement files complete for all transactions
- [ ] SAM.gov verification on file for all contractors and subrecipients
- [ ] Sole source justifications documented where applicable
- [ ] Contract provisions include required federal clauses
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest disclosures on file

**Subrecipient Monitoring (WCRP):**
- [ ] Subaward agreements contain all required elements
- [ ] Risk assessments completed for each subrecipient
- [ ] Monitoring activities documented (desk reviews, site visits)
- [ ] Subrecipient audit reports reviewed (if applicable)
- [ ] Management decisions issued on subrecipient findings (if applicable)

**Reporting:**
- [ ] All financial reports submitted on time
- [ ] All performance reports submitted on time
- [ ] Program income properly reported
- [ ] Real property and equipment inventories current

**Internal Controls:**
- [ ] Written policies and procedures in place for:
  - [ ] Financial management
  - [ ] Procurement
  - [ ] Travel
  - [ ] Time and effort
  - [ ] Subrecipient monitoring
  - [ ] Conflict of interest
  - [ ] Cash management
- [ ] Segregation of duties documented
- [ ] Internal control deficiencies from prior audits addressed

**Prior Audit Follow-Up:**
- [ ] Prior year findings reviewed
- [ ] Corrective actions implemented and documented
- [ ] Status of open findings summarized for auditor
