## 1. CDBG-DR Regulatory Framework

CDBG-DR (Community Development Block Grant — Disaster Recovery) is authorized under Title I of the Housing and Community Development Act of 1974, as amended. Disaster recovery funds are appropriated by Congress and administered by HUD through Federal Register Notices that establish program-specific requirements.

The compliance hierarchy for WCRP:
1. **Congressional appropriation** (e.g., Bipartisan Budget Act of 2018)
2. **Federal Register Notices** (HUD-specific CDBG-DR requirements)
3. **HUD regulations** (24 CFR Part 570)
4. **2 CFR Part 200** (Uniform Guidance — applies to CDBG-DR)
5. **PRDOH Action Plan** and amendments
6. **PRDOH policies and procedures** (as pass-through entity)
7. **Subrecipient agreement** terms (between PRDOH and FPR)

Where CDBG-DR program requirements conflict with 2 CFR 200, the more restrictive requirement applies unless HUD has issued a waiver.

## 2. National Objectives

Every CDBG-DR activity must meet one of three national objectives:

### Benefit to Low- and Moderate-Income (LMI) Persons
- **Area benefit:** Activity serves an area where at least 51% of residents are LMI
- **Limited clientele:** Activity benefits a specific group, at least 51% of whom are LMI
- **Housing:** Activity provides/improves housing occupied by LMI households
- **Job creation/retention:** Creates or retains jobs, at least 51% held by or available to LMI persons

### Aid in the Prevention or Elimination of Slums or Blight
- Area basis (designated slum/blight area)
- Spot basis (specific property)

### Urgent Need
- Conditions posing serious and immediate threat to health or safety
- The grantee cannot finance activities on its own
- Other financial resources are not available

For WCRP resilience planning: The primary national objective is typically LMI area benefit, where the plans serve communities with majority LMI populations. FPR and PRDOH must document how each plan/activity meets the applicable national objective.

## 3. Eligible Activities

CDBG-DR eligible activities for planning (relevant to WCRP):
- Planning activities (24 CFR 570.205) — Community development plans, policy planning, neighborhood plans, overall community development planning
- Technical assistance (24 CFR 570.206) — Capacity building for subrecipients and communities
- General program administration (24 CFR 570.206) — Management, coordination, monitoring, evaluation

Planning activities must be tied to disaster recovery and resilience. WCRP's 65 resilience planes fall squarely under this category.

## 4. Environmental Review (NEPA/HUD)

All CDBG-DR activities must comply with the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) and HUD's environmental review procedures at 24 CFR Part 58.

For planning activities: Generally exempt from environmental review under 24 CFR 58.34(a) (activities that do not alter physical conditions). However, if the planning leads to physical activities (construction, infrastructure), those subsequent activities require environmental review before funds are committed.

The Responsible Entity (RE) for environmental review is typically the grantee (PRDOH) or its designee. FPR should not commit funds to physical activities stemming from WCRP plans until environmental review is completed by the RE.

## 5. Davis-Bacon and Related Acts

**Applies to:** Construction contracts over $2,000 funded in whole or part with CDBG-DR funds.

Requirements:
- Pay prevailing wages as determined by the Department of Labor
- Submit weekly certified payrolls
- Post wage determinations at the work site
- Maintain records for 3 years after project completion

**FPR/WCRP relevance:** If any WCRP-funded activities include construction components (infrastructure improvements, facility rehabilitation), Davis-Bacon applies. For planning-only activities, Davis-Bacon does not apply, but FPR should be aware for any construction activities that may result from the plans.

## 6. Section 3 Economic Opportunities

Section 3 of the Housing and Urban Development Act of 1968 requires that employment and other economic opportunities generated by HUD financial assistance be directed, to the greatest extent feasible, to low- and very low-income persons and businesses.

**Applies when:** HUD-assisted projects involve housing rehabilitation, housing construction, or other public construction with more than $200,000 in HUD funds.

Requirements:
- Prioritize hiring Section 3 workers (residents of public housing, low-income persons in the metro area)
- Prioritize contracting with Section 3 business concerns
- Report Section 3 compliance through HUD's reporting system

## 7. Uniform Relocation Act (URA)

The Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Policies Act of 1970 (URA) applies to any CDBG-DR activity that involves:
- Acquisition of real property
- Displacement of persons (residential or commercial)
- Demolition of occupied structures

Requirements include: fair market value appraisals, relocation assistance, moving expenses, advisory services, and replacement housing payments.

**WCRP relevance:** Planning activities do not typically trigger URA. However, if plans recommend property acquisition or projects that would displace residents, the implementing entity must comply with URA.

## 8. Fair Housing and Civil Rights

CDBG-DR programs must comply with:
- **Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964** — No discrimination based on race, color, or national origin
- **Fair Housing Act** — Affirmatively further fair housing
- **Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act** — Accessibility for persons with disabilities
- **Age Discrimination Act of 1975**
- **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)**
- **Executive Orders 11246 and 13166** — EEO and limited English proficiency (LEP)

For Puerto Rico specifically: LEP requirements mean all program materials and outreach should be accessible in both English and Spanish. WCRP plans are developed in Spanish, which appropriately serves the community.

## 9. Citizen Participation

CDBG-DR requires meaningful citizen participation in the planning process:
- Public hearings and comment periods
- Accessible meetings (ADA-compliant locations, language access)
- Publication of plans and amendments for public review
- Response to citizen comments
- Anti-displacement and relocation plans

WCRP's community-based resilience planning process inherently fulfills citizen participation requirements. FPR should maintain documentation of all community engagement activities, meeting attendance, public comments received, and how comments were addressed.

## 10. Anti-Fraud, Waste, and Abuse

CDBG-DR programs have heightened scrutiny for fraud prevention:
- **Internal controls** must be established and documented
- **Whistleblower protections** for employees reporting fraud
- **Hotline reporting** — HUD OIG and PRDOH fraud reporting mechanisms
- **Mandatory disclosures** (§200.113) — FPR must disclose any violation of federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity
- **Subrecipient monitoring** — Heightened due to disaster recovery context

FPR must report any suspected fraud, waste, or abuse immediately to PRDOH and, as applicable, to HUD OIG.

## 11. Duplication of Benefits

The Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act prohibits the use of CDBG-DR funds for costs that have been or will be reimbursed by another source (FEMA, insurance, SBA disaster loans, other federal programs).

FPR and PRDOH must establish procedures to identify and prevent duplication of benefits for any individual, household, or entity receiving CDBG-DR assistance. This includes:
- Checking FEMA, SBA, and insurance records
- Requiring applicant certifications
- Subrogation agreements where applicable
- Monitoring for subsequent payments that would create duplication

For WCRP planning activities: Duplication of benefits is less of a direct concern for planning work, but FPR should ensure that planning costs are not also being funded by another source.

## 12. Financial Management — CDBG-DR Specifics

In addition to 2 CFR 200 financial management requirements:

### Drawdown Procedures
- Funds drawn from PRDOH must follow PRDOH's established drawdown schedule and procedures
- FPR must minimize time between drawdown and disbursement
- Supporting documentation must be maintained for each drawdown request

### Cost Categories Specific to CDBG-DR
- **Activity delivery costs** — Direct costs of carrying out CDBG-DR activities
- **Program administration** — Subject to the 5% cap on planning/administration (or as specified in the Federal Register Notice and PRDOH Action Plan)
- **Planning costs** — WCRP falls under this category

### Program Income under CDBG-DR
Program income must be returned to the CDBG-DR program and used before drawing additional funds. This is typically the "return" method for CDBG-DR, which differs from the general 2 CFR 200 default.

## 13. Reporting Requirements

### Financial Reporting
- SF-425 Federal Financial Report (or PRDOH-specified equivalent)
- Frequency as specified in subaward agreement
- Must reconcile to accounting records

### Performance Reporting
- QPR (Quarterly Performance Report) to HUD via DRGR (Disaster Recovery Grant Reporting) system
- Progress reports to PRDOH per subaward agreement
- Metrics: beneficiaries served, activities completed, national objectives achieved

### DRGR System
HUD's Disaster Recovery Grant Reporting (DRGR) system tracks CDBG-DR expenditures and accomplishments. PRDOH enters data into DRGR, but FPR must provide the underlying data to support PRDOH's reporting.

## 14. PRDOH as Pass-Through Entity

For WCRP, the relationship is:
- **HUD** → Federal awarding agency
- **PRDOH** → Recipient and pass-through entity
- **FPR** → Subrecipient
- **17 organizations** → Sub-subrecipients (if applicable) or contractors

PRDOH's responsibilities to FPR:
- Provide subaward with all required information (CFDA number, award terms, compliance requirements)
- Assess FPR's risk of noncompliance
- Monitor FPR's activities and expenditures
- Issue management decisions on any audit findings
- Provide technical assistance

FPR's obligations to PRDOH:
- Comply with all subaward terms and conditions
- Submit required reports on time
- Maintain records and make them available for review
- Cooperate with monitoring visits
- Disclose any noncompliance issues
- Ensure proper use of funds per the approved scope of work
